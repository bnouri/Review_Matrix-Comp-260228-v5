% ### Non-Intrusive Variability Analysis with Parallelism in Stochastic Space and Time Domain

% I developed an efficient method for **time-domain variability analysis** of circuits described by large systems of equations with multiple stochastic parameters. The proposed technique combines **Stochastic Collocation (SC)** with **Numerical Inversion of the Laplace Transform (NILT)**, enabling **embarrassingly parallel computation** in both the stochastic space and the time domain—an advantage not achievable with conventional time-stepping integration methods.

% The SC algorithm selects parameter samples using sparse grids, ensuring parsimonious coverage of the stochastic space. At each sample node, NILT is used to compute the time-domain response independently across time instances, eliminating the need for sequential time-marching and repeated interpolation. This structure allows full exploitation of parallel computing resources.

% Benchmark results confirm that the proposed method achieves **speedups of up to 150×** compared to standard Monte Carlo approaches based on trapezoidal integration [C1]. The method maintains high accuracy while significantly reducing simulation time, making it well-suited for large-scale variability-aware analysis in industrial design environments.

% These contributions enable scalable and robust transient analysis under uncertainty, supporting faster design iterations, improved reliability, and broader design space exploration in advanced circuit simulation workflows.

These contributions underwent extensive regression testing against industry standards and were successfully integrated into two market-leading EDA tools: **Spectre** and **Solido-AFS**.
